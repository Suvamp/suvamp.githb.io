About me

My path into GIS started with an environmental science degree and a lot of time outdoors. The further I got into remote sensing and satellite analysis, the more I wanted to understand the computational side — which led to my master's research on automated shoreline detection, a publication in a peer-reviewed journal, and a stint at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory working on wildfire risk in the Colombian Amazon.

These days I'm a GIS Analyst at Riverside County's Office of Economic Development, where I build dashboards and spatial tools that support real land use and economic decisions. I'm also finishing a CS post-baccalaureate at Oregon State — mostly because I want to understand what's actually happening inside the models I use.

My interest is at the intersection where spatial data meets machine learning. I like problems where the geography matters — where a result in one place genuinely differs from a result two miles away, and you need to account for that.

Tools I reach for: Python, ArcGIS Pro, Google Earth Engine, scikit-learn, PostgreSQL, JavaScript. I've also worked extensively with drone imagery, GNSS surveying, and the Census and AFDC APIs for demographic and infrastructure analysis.

Automated Shoreline Extraction
from Satellite Imagery

For my master's thesis I built a machine learning pipeline in Google Earth Engine to automatically map high-water shorelines from PlanetScope satellite imagery — the kind of work that used to require researchers walking beaches with GPS units. I validated results using UAV photogrammetry and GNSS surveys I conducted myself.

The paper was published in the International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation, and I presented the research at AGU Fall Meeting 2022 in Chicago.

Commercial Opportunity Zone Analysis
— Riverside County

Working in Riverside County's economic development office gave me access to questions I could actually test. This analysis pipelines Census ACS data, OpenStreetMap commercial POIs, and HUD Opportunity Zone designations through a spatial statistics workflow across all 438 county census tracts.

Moran's I of 0.68 confirmed that commercial activity clusters geographically — which matters for where you place investment. GWR revealed that the standard income-to-business-density relationship breaks down in the Coachella Valley, where tourism-driven demand operates independently of local income. Six distinct economic corridor types emerged from K-Means clustering. About 70% of HUD Opportunity Zone tracts independently landed in the top tercile of the data-driven score — a useful validation of the federal designations.

Python · GeoPandas · PySAL · OSMnx · mgwr · scikit-learn · Folium · Census API

EV Charging Site Suitability API
— California

Turned a static GIS suitability analysis into a working REST API. The FastAPI backend scores all 58 California counties in real time across four factors — population density, highway access, existing station gaps, and income equity — using min-max normalized multi-criteria analysis. Weights are user-adjustable.

The frontend is an ArcGIS Maps SDK choropleth map with live weight sliders and a county detail panel showing both composite scores and raw data. Auto-generated Swagger docs ship at /docs.
